A Dental Emergency for You
is Our Emergency, Too!

What can we do if we see you on an emergency basis? We will start right away by diagnosing the cause of your problem. Your dental emergency could result from something as simple as a stuck piece of food that can’t be removed with floss. We will find it, remove it and disinfect the area.

If your dental emergency consists of an abscess, we can relieve your pain and, if needed, provide you with antibiotics. This should improve your tooth pain until you can schedule a regular office visit for a more complete resolution.

A broken tooth may be able to be repaired and a loosened tooth can be secured in the jaw so healing can start.

When a person is seen by an emergency dentist quickly enough after knocking out a tooth, it might be possible to clean that tooth and restore it to the jaw bone. This action must take place within 30 minutes.

We have the skills and the facilities to repair your unexpected injury or dental damage and relieve your pain. Then we can schedule you for further appointments to complete your treatment.

If you have trouble finding an emergency dentist in Miami Beach, please call Family & Cosmetic Dentistry Wellness Spa in Bay Harbor Islands, FL. We will see a patient in pain even during evening hours.

What to Do Until You Can
Get Emergency Dentistry

  • Toothache: Floss gently to remove food particles, then make a mixture of salt and warm water. Rinse your mouth with this mixture repeatedly. Use an over-the-counter pain reliever as needed.
  • Broken tooth: If the tooth is bleeding, press gauze or a soft towel on the area until bleeding stops. Put a cold pack on the outside of your cheek to reduce pain.
  • Knocked out tooth: If possible, replace the tooth in its socket. If not, store the tooth in milk or hold it inside the cheek. Never touch the tooth by the root but just by the hard, white crown. Get to a dentist the first minute possible. If the tooth can be restored to its socket within 30 minutes, it may be able to be saved. It may still require root canal therapy afterwards.
  • Lost crown or bridge: Purchase dental cement at a pharmacy. (NEVER use Superglue or any other glue.) Place this cement inside the crown or bridge and fit it back in place. Bite down gently when the crown or bridge seems to fit well. Wipe away any cement that gets squeezed out. Note: A damaged crown or bridge may not be able to be cemented back in place.
  • Lost filling: To protect a tooth after a filling has been lost, spread dental cement across the damaged area to protect it. Avoid chewing in this area.

Polishing, Too

When you get your teeth cleaned at your dentist’s office, it’s likely that your hygienist uses hand tools and a sonic cleaner on all the surfaces of your teeth and then follows this with polishing. The AIRFLOW system does two steps at once, incorporating both sonic cleaning and polishing. Completing two types of cleaning in one step saves you time. You may also find the AIRFLOW cleaning system more comfortable than earlier types of dental cleaning you’ve had. A dental hygienist typically follows AIRFLOW cleaning with manual cleaning of any tough, hardened spots of calculus.
